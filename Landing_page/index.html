Benefits

Lemon

Lemons are an excellent source of vitamin C, which is essential for the immune system, skin health, and the absorption of iron from plant-based foods.
Lemons contain antioxidants, such as flavonoids, which help neutralize free radicals in the body. Free radicals can contribute to chronic diseases and aging.

Mint

Mint is known for its ability to soothe indigestion and alleviate symptoms such as gas and bloating. Peppermint oil, in particular, has been studied for its potential to relieve irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) symptoms.
The antibacterial properties of mint can help freshen breath. Chewing mint leaves or consuming mint-infused products may contribute to improved oral hygiene.

Pineapple

Pineapple is a hydrating fruit that contains water and electrolytes, such as potassium. Maintaining proper hydration and electrolyte balance is essential for various bodily functions.
Pineapple contains beta-carotene, which is converted into vitamin A in the body. Vitamin A is essential for maintaining good vision and eye health.

Honey

Honey is a delicious and natural alternative to refined sugars. It can be used as a sweetener in beverages, baked goods, and various recipes.
In small amounts, honey may have prebiotic effects, promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria in the gut. This can contribute to overall digestive health.
